Drag to annotate

Select text and it's instantly highlighted or underlined. No hotkeys, no Markdown edits.

🎨

Editable palette

Five vivid defaults, a “+” to add named colours, and a hover “×” to delete — right from the palette.

💡

Neon & brighter

Give highlights a luminous neon glow, or make underlines pop with a brighter, vivid line.

↩️

Undo / redo

Ctrl/Cmd+Z removes your last annotation; Shift to redo. Per-note history, right in Reading view.

Roomier touch targets, tap to select, long-press for options. The toolbar stays put through rotations.

Fast, durable storage

Per-file shards with lazy loading and indexes — incremental writes, low memory, still synced with your vault.

Weekly research notes

Large language models have shifted from research curiosities to everyday tools. The most capable systems now reason across long documents, call external tools, and hold context over an entire working session. What used to take a bespoke pipeline is increasingly a single well-phrased request.

Retrieval still matters. Grounding a model in your own notes keeps answers specific and checkable, and it turns a general assistant into something that actually knows your project. The interesting design question is no longer whether to retrieve, but what to retrieve and how much context is worth its cost.

The best interface is the one that disappears — you think about the work, not the tool.
  • Annotations should never rewrite the source note.
  • Colour is information: reserve one hue per theme.
  • Undo must be instant, and forgiving.

Reading actively — marking the claims that matter, underlining the caveats — is how a long note becomes a durable idea. Highlight the sentence above about interfaces, or underline “what to retrieve”, and see how it feels.

Install in under a minute

  1. 1

    Download the plugin

    Grab the ready-to-install folder as a zip.

    ⬇ Download inkless-highlighter.zip
  2. 2

    Unzip into your vault

    Extract it so the folder lands at:

    <your-vault>/.obsidian/plugins/inkless-highlighter/

    It should contain manifest.json, main.js and styles.css.

  3. 3

    Enable it

    In Obsidian: Settings → Community plugins, turn off Restricted mode if asked, then enable Inkless Highlighter. Open a note in Reading view and start highlighting.
